Samawah

The city of Samawah is surrounded by a large area of palm plantations, which are famous in Iraq. There is also an Iraqi folk song entitled "Nakhl al Samawah". Near the city lies a deep salt lake, called Lake Sawa. The city was a center of Japanese troops after the invasion of Iraq in 2003. Its objective was to re-establish the region's infrastructure in 2005-2006.

Al-Rumaitha

Al-Rumaitha is an Iraqi city and a district center in the province of Muthanna in southern Iraq. The population of the city is estimated at about 115,000. The judiciary has a population of more than 328,000. Al-Rumaitha is located on a branch of the Euphrates River.

Salman district

Salman district is one of the administrative districts of Iraq to the province of Muthanna and its center is the city of Salman, south-west of the city of Samawah and the second largest district in Iraq after the district of al-Ruba in Anbar province, an area of 46 928 km 2 and has a population of about 19 thousand people,
